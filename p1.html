Double Five

Previous Next

As part of our first multidisciplinary project, we created a game using two dice with the following rules: each player rolls the two dice simultaneously in turn. The player then adds the total of the two dice to the total of the previous player. If this sum is less than 55, the player is saved and can pass the dice on to the next player. However, if the sum equals or exceeds 55, the player is eliminated, and the other player is declared the winner.

Our five-member team worked together to complete this project. The project was divided into two distinct parts: electronics and programming. I was responsible for the programming part.

To implement the electronics, we used a breadboard, while for the programming, we developed the code in C language. To ensure a harmonious link between the breadboard and the program, we integrated a communication board.

It was a rewarding experience, giving me the opportunity to hone my programming skills while developing my ability to collaborate effectively within a group.
