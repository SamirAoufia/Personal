Who am I?


My name is Samir Aoufia, I am 22 years old and I live in Gosselies, Belgium. I have dual Algerian and Belgian nationality. I speak 2 languages French which is my native language and English. I'm currently in my last year of industrial computing at HelHa "Haute Ecole de Louvain en Hainaut" in Charleroi, and I'm enrolled in a bridging program for my master in computer science at UMons, also in Charleroi. I work as a student employee at the Smart Gastronomy Lab, Gembloux Agro-Bio Tech, ULiège.


I spent my childhood in Gosselies, where I learned invaluable life lessons. Even though I didn't grow up with the best neighbourhoods, it allowed me to gain a valuable and unique educational experience. I think it shows that even in less privileged circumstances, I'm still building my career path.


I have always liked challenges. When I had to choose a career path, I looked for a field that would allow me to face new challenges every day. That's why I chose IT.


My bachelor's degree in industrial IT provided me solid foundations in software development, system administration, automation and IoT. The projects I carried out during my studies, which combined theory and practice, have strengthened my ability to solve problems in real-world scenarios.


For now, my current goal is to get my bachelor's degree. This is the first step in my career as a computer scientist. After achieving it, my next objective will be getting a master's degree in computer science to deepen my knowledge and skills in IT, which will lead to many new opportunities in this sector.


Once I have completed my master's degree, I would like to get a challenging job. In a professional environment that offers both stability and diversity, I would be able to continue developing my IT skills together with facing challenges. The idea of being constantly stimulated and able to surpass myself is essential for me to maintain my passion and motivation at work.
